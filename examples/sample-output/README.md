# Sample output — what the DevLoop chain produces

These files illustrate the **shape** of DevLoop's artifacts for a small feature
("self-service partner SFTP onboarding"), grounded in the bundled
[`../integrations-src/`](../integrations-src/) sources (SSO, Transactional Email, SFTP):

- [`brd.md`](brd.md) — *(optional phase)* a Business Requirements Document: business objectives
  (`OBJ-n`) + numbered business requirements (`BR-n`) with sign-off (the Business Analyst's output).
- [`requirements.md`](requirements.md) — numbered FR/NFR, each tagged with the wiki concept +
  source **and the `BR-n` it serves** (the Requirements Analyst's output).
- [`stories.md`](stories.md) — epics → INVEST user stories → Gherkin acceptance criteria +
  a traceability matrix (the Story Writer's output).
- [`story-review.md`](story-review.md) — an independent INVEST / Definition-of-Ready / coverage
  pass with a findings table (the Story Reviewer's output).
- [`jira-plan.md`](jira-plan.md) — BA-vs-TECH project split, Components from the wikis, field
  mappings, and a per-story Jira mapping (the Jira Organizer's output).

## The full chain, end to end
With a BRD in play, every story traces up to a business requirement and objective — and down to a
Jira issue. The whole chain for one thread (SFTP key-based auth):

```
source: integrations/SFTP [S3] — "directory convention; key-based access"
   └─ BR-3  no secrets over insecure channels           (brd.md, serves OBJ-2)
        └─ FR-3  register SSH key; no emailed passwords  (requirements.md)
             └─ US-3  Register SSH key (no emailed secret) + Gherkin AC  (stories.md)
                  └─ reviewed ✓  (story-review.md)
                       └─ TECH / Story / Component: SFTP / Highest  (jira-plan.md)
```

Every row of the `OBJ → BR → FR → US` matrix:

| Objective | Business req (brd.md) | Functional req (requirements.md) | Story (stories.md) | Jira (jira-plan.md) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OBJ-2 | BR-2 (use company identity) | FR-1 (SSO login) | US-1 | TECH/Story · SSO |
| OBJ-1, OBJ-3 | BR-1 (self-serve, no ticket) | FR-2 (create SFTP space) | US-2 | TECH/Story · SFTP |
| OBJ-2 | BR-3 (no secrets over insecure channels) | FR-3 (SSH key), FR-4 (no-secret email) | US-3, US-4 | TECH/Story · SFTP, Email |
| OBJ-2 | BR-4 (auditable) | FR-5 (audit trail) | US-5 | TECH/Story · SFTP |
| OBJ-3 | BR-5 (Ops can revoke) | FR-6 (`Could`) | — (deferred) | — |

> **Illustrative, hand-authored** to show the format and the grounding/traceability style — not
> a captured model run. Your real artifacts are generated by the agent against *your* wikis, so
> the wording and depth will differ. They follow the same templates in `core/shared/`. The BRD is
> the optional first phase (the Business Analyst → `brd.md`); agile teams skip it and start at the Requirements Analyst.
